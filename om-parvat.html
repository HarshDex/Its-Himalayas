The Sacred Sign

Where Nature Writes
OM in Snow

Om Parvat (6191m) is a sacred Himalayan peak in Pithoragarh district, Uttarakhand. What makes it truly extraordinary — and unlike any mountain on earth — is the permanent snow formation on its face that forms a near-perfect 'ॐ' (OM) symbol, visible to the naked eye from the Nabhidhang viewpoint.

This phenomenon is considered a divine blessing by Hindu pilgrims, who flock here as a more accessible alternative to Kailash Mansarovar in Tibet. The sight of the OM symbol on the mountain face, framed by blue Himalayan sky, is a moment that pilgrims describe as genuinely transformative.

Our 7-day journey is designed around getting you to the best viewpoints in perfect conditions — with proper acclimatization, skilled guides, and zero-waste camps.

Questions & Answers

FAQs — Om Parvat Trek

The OM snow symbol is typically visible from May to October when skies are clear. The best visibility is in May–June (pre-monsoon) when snow cover is fresh and clear. We always plan the day at Nabhidhang to maximise visibility odds.
Om Parvat is a shorter, slightly easier trek with a lower max altitude (3740m vs 4800m). The main attraction is the unique OM snow symbol. Adi Kailash is more physically demanding but includes the sacred Parvati Sarovar lake. Many travellers do both on our combo tour.
Om Parvat is rated moderate and is suitable for first-time Himalayan trekkers with decent fitness. You should be able to walk 12–14km per day. We recommend 3–4 weeks of daily walks (5km+) before the trip.
